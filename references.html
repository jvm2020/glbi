Parliamentary Budget Officer (PBO) Reports

2025: A Distributional Analysis of a National Guaranteed Basic Income – Update (February 2025)

Latest comprehensive analysis examining poverty reduction (up to 40%), costs ($53-107 billion gross, $3.6-5 billion net), and impacts on work incentives.

Link: https://www.pbo-dpb.ca/en/publications/RP-2425-029-S

PDF: https://publications.gc.ca/collections/collection_2025/dpb-pbo/YN5-220-2025-eng.pdf

2021: Distributional and Fiscal Analysis of a National Guaranteed Basic Income

Foundational PBO report using parameters from Ontario's 2017 basic income pilot. Estimated lowest income quintile would see average $4,535 increase (+17.5% per year).

Link: https://www.pbo-dpb.ca/en/publications/RP-2122-001-S

2021: Distributional and Fiscal Analysis of a National Guaranteed Basic Income – A Focus on Canadians with a Disability

Specialized analysis examining how a national GLBI would impact Canadians with disabilities, including interactions with existing disability supports.

Link: https://www.pbo-dpb.ca/en/publications/RP-2122-014-S

2020: Update: Five-Year Cost Estimate of the Guaranteed Basic Income

Five-year projection of costs for implementing a guaranteed basic income program in Canada.

Link: https://www.pbo-dpb.ca/en/publications/RP-2021-014-S

2020: Costing a Guaranteed Basic Income During the COVID-19 Pandemic

Emergency analysis of implementing a guaranteed basic income as a response to the economic impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic.

Link: https://www.pbo-dpb.ca/en/publications/RP-2021-001-S

2018: Costing a National Guaranteed Basic Income Using the Ontario Basic Income Model

First major PBO analysis examining the costs of scaling up Ontario's Basic Income Pilot model to a national program.

Link: https://www.pbo-dpb.ca/en/publications/RP-1819-026-S

Ontario Basic Income Pilot & Provincial Studies

2019: Southern Ontario's Basic Income Experience

Institution: McMaster University

Authors: Wayne Lewchuk, Marlea Clarke, and Allyson de Wolff

Comprehensive study examining the experiences of participants in Ontario's Basic Income Pilot (2017-2018) before it was prematurely cancelled. Documents impacts on food security, housing stability, health outcomes, and employment.

Link: McMaster Labour Studies PDF

Ontario Basic Income Pilot (2017-2018)

Ontario's three-year basic income pilot project, launched in 2017 and cancelled in 2018. Provided up to $16,989/year for single adults and $24,027/year for couples, minus 50% of any earned income.

Government Archive: Ontario.ca

Basic Income: A Radical Proposal for a Free Society and a Sane Economy

Authors: Philippe Van Parijs and Yannick Vanderborght

Publisher: Harvard University Press

One of the most comprehensive analyses and defenses of UBI, covering philosophical, economic, and political questions. Examines funding, transition strategies, and practical policy frameworks, with global examples including Canada's Mincome experiment.

Link: Google Books

Give People Money: How a Universal Basic Income Would End Poverty, Revolutionize Work, and Remake the World

Journalistic take on how UBI could transform societies, citing experiments from around the world.

Utopia for Realists: How We Can Build the Ideal World

Author: Rutger Bregman

Explores big ideas for a better society, with UBI as a major component, examining historical precedents and possible futures.
