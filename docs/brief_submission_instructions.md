# How to Submit a Brief to Parliament

This guide outlines the process for submitting a brief to a parliamentary committee in support of Guaranteed Livable Basic Income (GLBI) legislation.

## What is a Brief?

A brief is a written submission to a parliamentary committee that presents your views, concerns, or recommendations on a specific bill or issue under committee study.

## Who Can Submit a Brief?

- Individual Canadians
- Organizations
- Advocacy groups
- Businesses
- Academic institutions
- Any interested party with relevant perspectives

## When to Submit

Briefs are typically accepted when:
- A bill is referred to committee for study
- A committee is conducting a study on a relevant topic
- The committee has issued a call for submissions

Check the committee websites for specific deadlines and calls for briefs.

## Brief Format and Content

### Required Elements

1. **Contact Information**
   - Your name or organization name
   - Province/territory
   - Email or phone (optional)

2. **Subject Line**
   - Clear indication of which bill or study you're addressing
   - Example: "Brief on Bill S-206 - Guaranteed Livable Basic Income"

3. **Position Statement**
   - Concise statement of your position (support, oppose, or suggest amendments)
   - 1-2 sentences summarizing your main point

4. **Main Content**
   - Your arguments, evidence, or recommendations
   - Personal stories or case studies (if applicable)
   - Data or research findings
   - Specific amendments you propose

### Formatting Guidelines

- **Length**: Typically 5-10 pages (check specific committee requirements)
- **Language**: English or French (bilingual submissions welcome)
- **Format**: PDF is generally preferred
- **Font**: Clear, readable font (11-12 pt)
- **Structure**: Use headings and bullet points for clarity

### Tips for Effective Briefs

1. **Be Specific**: Reference specific sections of the bill
2. **Provide Evidence**: Support claims with data, research, or examples
3. **Be Concise**: Make your points clearly and directly
4. **Be Respectful**: Use professional, respectful language
5. **Propose Solutions**: If you identify problems, suggest alternatives
6. **Tell Your Story**: Personal experiences can be powerful (if relevant)

## How to Submit

### 1. Identify the Relevant Committee

For GLBI-related bills:
- **House of Commons**: Standing Committee on Human Resources, Skills and Social Development and the Status of Persons with Disabilities (HUMA)
- **Senate**: Standing Senate Committee on Social Affairs, Science and Technology

### 2. Prepare Your Brief

- Use the GLBI Canada app or your own document
- Include all required elements
- Review for clarity and accuracy
- Save as PDF

### 3. Submit to Committee Clerk

**House of Commons Committees:**
- Email to the committee clerk
- Find committee contact information at: https://www.ourcommons.ca/Committees/en/Home
- Subject line should include bill number (e.g., "Brief for Bill S-206")

**Senate Committees:**
- Email to the Senate committee clerk
- Find committee contact information at: https://sencanada.ca/en/committees/
- Follow any specific submission instructions on the committee page

### 4. Follow Specific Committee Instructions

Each committee may have specific requirements:
- Submission deadlines
- Format preferences
- Length limits
- Additional required information

**Always check the committee's website for the most current submission guidelines.**

## After Submitting

1. **Confirmation**: You should receive confirmation that your brief was received
2. **Publication**: Briefs are typically published on the committee website
3. **Testimony**: You may be invited to appear before the committee (optional)
4. **Follow-Up**: Monitor the committee's proceedings to see if your brief is referenced

## Additional Ways to Engage

Beyond submitting briefs:
- Contact your Member of Parliament directly
- Attend public committee meetings (in-person or virtual)
- Participate in committee consultations
- Join or support GLBI advocacy organizations
- Share information with your community
